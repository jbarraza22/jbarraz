Perch: workspace discovery app

Find your perch – a space to land, work, and connect

Led user research and data analysis for a mobile workspace discovery application that reduced search time by 75% and increased user satisfaction from 52% to 89%. Applied mixed-methods research with 56 participants to translate user needs into actionable requirements that guided the design team in creating a solution aligned with business goals and user expectations.

Problem statement

Who

Remote workers and students seeking productive workspaces

What

Wasted hours searching for workspaces, only to discover missing essentials like reliable Wi‑Fi or power outlets, disrupting productivity

Outcome

Verified amenities enable workspace discovery in under 2 minutes, reducing search time by 75% and increasing satisfaction from 52% to 89%

Understanding user needs

Applied Enterprise Design Thinking principles to understand user needs and translate research findings into actionable requirements that informed the solution design.

User research findings

  • Users needed standardized information about Wi‑Fi quality, outlet availability, and noise level
  • Proximity and Wi‑Fi quality emerged as primary decision-making factors
  • Community-driven recommendations increased trust and discoverability
  • Existing platforms buried work-friendly attributes under extended menus

Requirements translation

Analyzed research data from 56 participants to identify critical workspace attributes and user priorities. Translated findings into clear specifications for:

  • Information architecture structure
  • Feature prioritization framework
  • User interface requirements
  • Content strategy guidelines

Approach

Applied Enterprise Design Thinking methods to understand user needs, evaluate solutions, and collaborate with cross-functional teams to deliver a technology solution aligned with business objectives.

Contextual inquiry

Conducted interviews to understand workspace selection factors. Analyzed data to identify themes and pain points.

Tree testing

Evaluated information architecture to optimize navigation. Translated findings into design specifications.

Usability testing

Tested prototype with 56 participants. Documented behaviors and issues to inform iterative development.

Team collaboration

Worked with designers and developers to translate research into actionable requirements.

Solution implementation

Translated research findings into a technology solution that addressed user needs while supporting business objectives through collaborative development.

Wi‑Fi quality ratings

Real-time ratings from users at each location for reliable connection assessment, addressing a critical user need identified through research.

Noise environment indicators

Clear visual indicators for focused environments or collaborative spaces, based on user feedback about workspace selection factors.

Power availability details

Specific information on outlet locations and availability for device charging, directly addressing user pain points discovered in research.

Community coworking

Features that enable users to coordinate casual co-working sessions at local spots, supporting the community-driven approach users valued.
